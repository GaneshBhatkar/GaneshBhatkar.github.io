Research Proposal

Title

Outdoor Autonomous 3-Wheeled Trike UGV: Active SLAM and Robust Safe Motion Control for Smart City Mobility

Abstract

Urban environments increasingly require agile, cost-effective autonomous ground vehicles (UGVs) to enable Smart City applications such as last-mile delivery, safety patrols, and real-time environmental monitoring. This research proposes the design and development of a 3-wheeled trike-type outdoor UGV, combining the maneuverability of a motorbike configuration with the passive stability needed for real-world urban deployment.

The system will integrate ROS 2, Edge AI (Jetson Nano), Active GNSS-Visual SLAM, and robust safe motion control strategies to achieve reliable outdoor navigation in semi-structured environments. Through field validation inspired by real-world urban scenarios, this project will contribute an open-source, reproducible platform for urban mobile robot research, aligning with Japan’s Smart City and urban mobility goals.

Introduction

As cities become smarter and more connected, autonomous mobile robots must navigate complex outdoor environments safely and efficiently. 4-wheeled UGVs offer stability but may struggle in tight spaces; 2-wheeled robots require active balancing, increasing control complexity.

A 3-wheeled trike-type configuration, inspired by motorbike designs, provides a balance of maneuverability, passive stability, and mechanical simplicity — well suited for urban sidewalks, campus paths, and service lanes.

Key challenges remain in:

  • Reliable outdoor localization in GNSS-limited “urban canyon” areas,
  • Active exploration and mapping with minimal drift,
  • Robust obstacle detection and real-time avoidance,
  • Safe trajectory tracking under uncertain outdoor conditions.

This project directly addresses these challenges by combining Active SLAM, robust safe motion control, and practical field testing for safe autonomous exploration.

Literature Review

  • Hayashibara et al. (2018) demonstrated GNSS-aided Lidar localization for outdoor mobile robots, providing practical methods for reliable field robot navigation under variable signal conditions.
  • The Tsukuba Challenge has established a public benchmark for real-world autonomous navigation, encouraging teams to develop robust UGVs that navigate urban-like public routes with dynamic obstacles.
  • Yamaha and Piaggio have developed 3-wheeled leaning trikes for urban maneuverability; however, these commercial vehicles are designed for human riders and lack autonomous capabilities.
  • Many ROS-based UGV research projects focus on differential-drive or skid-steer designs, with few open-source examples that combine a compact trike layout with Edge AI and robust outdoor localization for Smart City deployment.

Research Objectives

  1. Design and fabricate a 3-wheeled trike-type outdoor chassis with reliable steering and drive systems.
  2. Develop low-level real-time control with an STM32 MCU and micro-ROS, enabling safe actuator operation.
  3. Implement Active SLAM combining GNSS, visual SLAM, and Lidar for robust localization and information-gain exploration.
  4. Apply robust safe motion control strategies to maintain stable, accurate trajectory tracking in dynamic outdoor settings.
  5. Use Edge AI (Jetson Nano) for real-time perception tasks like object detection, SLAM, and path planning.
  6. Conduct outdoor field trials in urban-like test routes to validate performance under Smart City conditions.
  7. Release hardware design files, ROS 2 packages, and collected field data as open-source resources, supporting reproducible research in urban outdoor autonomy.

Methodology

  1. Hardware:
    • 3-wheeled chassis: front steering wheel, two rear drive wheels.
    • Motorized steering fork, BLDC hub motors, and swing-arm suspension for rough terrain.
    • Air-filled scooter wheels for outdoor durability.
  2. Embedded Control:
    • STM32 microcontroller with micro-ROS bridge for tight integration.
    • Safe motion control loops for reliable and stable operation.
  3. Perception & Computation:
    • Jetson Nano Edge AI.
    • GNSS module fused with visual SLAM.
    • Stereo camera and 2D/3D Lidar.
    • IMU and encoders for robust sensor fusion.
  4. Software Stack:
    • ROS 2, Navigation 2 stack.
    • RTAB-Map or ORB-SLAM2 for Active SLAM.
    • Information-gain exploration strategies for efficient coverage.
  5. Validation:
    • Simulations in Gazebo.
    • Indoor and outdoor field trials inspired by Tsukuba Challenge.
    • Urban mobility metrics: drift, trajectory accuracy, real-time obstacle avoidance.

Expected Outcomes

  • Fully functional outdoor trike UGV for Smart City testbeds.
  • Reproducible open-source hardware and software.
  • Datasets for benchmarking Active SLAM and safe motion control in outdoor urban conditions.
  • Strong contribution to future Smart City mobile robot development.

Future Scope

The modular platform can expand to support:

  • Parcel delivery
  • Safety and security patrol
  • Environmental monitoring
  • Tourist information services

This modular approach will enable deployment in multiple Smart City scenarios, supporting local governments and academic testbeds.
